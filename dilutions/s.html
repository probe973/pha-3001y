Dilutions - Solutions

Solutions to Dilutions Questions

Below are the step-by-step solutions for dilution problems.

Solutions

1. A solution contains $10\text{g}$ of solute per $100\text{mL}$ of solution. $200\text{mL}$ of this solution is diluted by adding a further $300\text{mL}$ of water. What is the resulting percentage strength of the new solution expressed as $\%$ w/v?

Initial concentration: $(10\text{g} \div 100\text{mL}) \times 100\% = 10\%$ w/v

Amount of solute in $200\text{mL}$: $10\%\text{ of }200\text{mL} = (10 \div 100) \times 200\text{mL} = 20\text{g}$

New total volume: $200\text{mL} + 300\text{mL} = 500\text{mL}$

New concentration: $(20\text{g} \div 500\text{mL}) \times 100\% = 4\%$ w/v

Answer: $\textbf{4.00\% w/v}$
