Fractions and Percentages - Solutions

Solutions to Fractions and Percentages Questions

Below are the step-by-step solutions to the fractions and percentages questions.

Solutions

1. Express $0.62$ as a percentage.

$0.62 \times 100 = 62$

Answer: $\textbf{62\%}$

2. Express $7.1$ as a fraction in its simplest form.

$7.1 = \frac{71}{10}$

Answer: $\mathbf{\frac{71}{10}}$

3. Express $450$ out of $3200$ as a percentage.

$(450 \div 3200) \times 100$

$450 \div 3200 = 0.140625$

$0.140625 \times 100 = 14.0625$

Answer: $\textbf{14.06\%}$

4. If you were travelling at $65$ miles per hour and decreased $8\%$ how fast would you be travelling?

$8\%$ of $65$: $(8 \times 65) \div 100 = 5.2$

$65 - 5.2 = 59.8$

Answer: $\textbf{59.80 \text{mph}}$

5. If your weekly wages were $£520$ and you received a $3\%$ pay increase, what would you earn each week (to the nearest $£1$)?

$3\%$ of $£520$: $(3 \times 520) \div 100 = 15.60$

$£520 + £15.60 = £535.60$

Answer: $\textbf{£536}$

6. If you had $300\text{g}$ of a cream which contained $7\%$ w/w active ingredient, how many grams of active ingredient would be present?

$7\%$ of $300\text{g}$: $(7 \times 300) \div 100 = 21$

Answer: $\textbf{21\text{g}}$

7. Express $0.15$ as a percentage.

$0.15 \times 100 = 15$

Answer: $\textbf{15\%}$

8. Express $3.2$ as a fraction in its simplest form.

$3.2 = \frac{32}{10}$

Simplify: $\frac{32 \div 2}{10 \div 2} = \frac{16}{5}$

Answer: $\mathbf{\frac{16}{5}}$

9. Express $150$ out of $1250$ as a percentage.

$(150 \div 1250) \times 100$

$150 \div 1250 = 0.12$

$0.12 \times 100 = 12$

Answer: $\textbf{12\%}$

10. If a car's fuel efficiency of $45$ miles per gallon decreased by $6\%$, what is the new fuel efficiency?

$6\%$ of $45$: $(6 \times 45) \div 100 = 2.7$

$45 - 2.7 = 42.3$

Answer: $\textbf{42.30 \text{mpg}}$

11. If a monthly bill was $£85$ and it increased by $5\%$, what would be the new monthly bill (to the nearest $£1$)?

$5\%$ of $£85$: $(5 \times 85) \div 100 = 4.25$

$£85 + £4.25 = £89.25$

Answer: $\textbf{£89}$

12. A solution contains $9\%$ active ingredient. How many milligrams would be in a $5\text{mL}$ dose?

Assume $1\text{mL}$ of solution weighs $1\text{g}$. So $5\text{mL} = 5\text{g}$.

$5\text{g} = 5000\text{mg}$

$9\%$ of $5000\text{mg}$: $(9 \times 5000) \div 100 = 450$

Answer: $\textbf{450\text{mg}}$

13. Express $0.009$ as a percentage.

$0.009 \times 100 = 0.9$

Answer: $\textbf{0.9\%}$

14. Express $1.6$ as a fraction in its simplest form.

$1.6 = \frac{16}{10}$

Simplify: $\frac{16 \div 2}{10 \div 2} = \frac{8}{5}$

Answer: $\mathbf{\frac{8}{5}}$

15. Express $210$ out of $1800$ as a percentage.

$(210 \div 1800) \times 100$

$210 \div 1800 = 0.11666...$

$0.11666... \times 100 = 11.666...$

Answer: $\textbf{11.67\%}$

16. If a population of $12000$ increased by $7\%$, what is the new population?

$7\%$ of $12000$: $(7 \times 12000) \div 100 = 840$

$12000 + 840 = 12840$

Answer: $\textbf{12840}$

17. If your annual income was $£32000$ and you received a $2\%$ pay cut, what would your new annual income be (to the nearest $£1$)?

$2\%$ of $£32000$: $(2 \times 32000) \div 100 = 640$

$£32000 - £640 = £31360$

Answer: $\textbf{£31360}$

18. If you had $150\text{mL}$ of a liquid which contained $4\%$ v/v active ingredient, how many $\text{mL}$ of active ingredient would be present?

$4\%$ of $150\text{mL}$: $(4 \times 150) \div 100 = 6$

Answer: $\textbf{6\text{mL}}$

19. Express $550$ out of $4200$ as a percentage.

$(550 \div 4200) \times 100$

$550 \div 4200 = 0.13095...$

$0.13095... \times 100 = 13.095...$

Answer: $\textbf{13.10\%}$

20. If a quantity of $280\text{g}$ is decreased by $11\%$, what is the new quantity in $\text{g}$?

$11\%$ of $280\text{g}$: $(11 \times 280) \div 100 = 30.8$

$280 - 30.8 = 249.2$

Answer: $\textbf{249.20\text{g}}$
