Molar Equations - Solutions

Solutions to Molar Equations Questions

Below are the step-by-step solutions for molar equation problems.

Solutions

1. How many grams of potassium chloride (molecular weight = $74.5$) are required to produce $500$ millilitres of a $3$ molar solution?

Volume in Litres: $500\text{mL} \div 1000 = 0.5\text{L}$

Moles required: Molarity $\times$ Volume (L) = $3\text{mol/L} \times 0.5\text{L} = 1.5\text{mol}$

Grams required: Moles $\times$ Molecular Weight = $1.5\text{mol} \times 74.5\text{g/mol} = 111.75\text{g}$

Answer: $\textbf{111.75\text{g}}$
