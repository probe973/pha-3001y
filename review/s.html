Additional percentage problems:

  1. If the price of a drug costs $£85$ per batch and increases in price by $5\%$, what is the new batch price?
    Increase: $5\%\text{ of }£85 = \frac{5}{100} \times 85 = 4.25$ New price: $£85 + £4.25 = £89.25$
    Solution: £89.25
  2. The value of some science equipment has decreased by $4.6\%$ this year. The value was $£2500$, so what is it now?
    Decrease: $4.6\%\text{ of }£2500 = \frac{4.6}{100} \times 2500 = 115$ New value: $£2500 - £115 = £2385$
    Solution: £2385.00
  3. A $145\text{cm}$ child has increased their height by $6\%$ over the year. What is their new height, to the nearest $\text{cm}$?
    Increase: $6\%\text{ of }145\text{cm} = \frac{6}{100} \times 145 = 8.7\text{cm}$ New height: $145\text{cm} + 8.7\text{cm} = 153.7\text{cm}$ Rounded to nearest cm: $154\text{cm}$
    Solution: 154 cm

Conversions

Convert the following into the given unit:

  1. $7.25\text{g}$ into $\text{mg}$
    $1\text{g} = 1000\text{mg}$ $7.25\text{g} \times 1000 = 7250\text{mg}$
    Solution: 7250 mg
  2. $0.0444\text{mg}$ into $\text{mcg}$
    $1\text{mg} = 1000\text{mcg}$ $0.0444\text{mg} \times 1000 = 44.4\text{mcg}$
    Solution: 44.4 mcg
  3. $93400\text{mL}$ into $\text{L}$
    $1\text{L} = 1000\text{mL}$ $93400\text{mL} \div 1000 = 93.4\text{L}$
    Solution: 93.4 L
  4. $65000\text{ng}$ into $\text{mcg}$
    $1\text{mcg} = 1000\text{ng}$ $65000\text{ng} \div 1000 = 65\text{mcg}$
    Solution: 65 mcg
  5. $0.000234\text{g}$ into $\text{mcg}$
    $1\text{g} = 1,000,000\text{mcg}$ $0.000234\text{g} \times 1,000,000 = 234\text{mcg}$
    Solution: 234 mcg
  6. $250000\text{mg}$ into $\text{kg}$
    $1\text{kg} = 1,000,000\text{mg}$ $250000\text{mg} \div 1,000,000 = 0.25\text{kg}$
    Solution: 0.25 kg
  7. $45\text{g}$ into $\text{ng}$
    $1\text{g} = 1,000,000,000\text{ng} = 10^9\text{ng}$ $45\text{g} \times 10^9 = 4.5 \times 10^{10}\text{ng}$
    Solution: $4.5 \times 10^{10}\text{ng}$
  8. $123\text{mcg}$ into $\text{g}$
    $1\text{g} = 1,000,000\text{mcg}$ $123\text{mcg} \div 1,000,000 = 0.000123\text{g}$ Or in scientific notation: $1.23 \times 10^{-4}\text{g}$
    Solution: 0.000123 g or $1.23 \times 10^{-4}\text{g}$
  9. $4.6 \times 10^8 \text{mg}$ into $\text{g}$
    $1\text{g} = 10^3\text{mg}$ $(4.6 \times 10^8\text{mg}) \div 10^3 = 4.6 \times 10^{(8-3)}\text{g} = 4.6 \times 10^5\text{g}$
    Solution: $4.6 \times 10^5\text{g}$
  10. $2.1 \times 10^{15} \text{mm}$ into $\text{km}$
    $1\text{km} = 10^6\text{mm}$ $(2.1 \times 10^{15}\text{mm}) \div 10^6 = 2.1 \times 10^{(15-6)}\text{km} = 2.1 \times 10^9\text{km}$
    Solution: $2.1 \times 10^9\text{km}$
  11. $5.77 \times 10^{-5} \text{L}$ into $\text{mL}$
    $1\text{L} = 10^3\text{mL}$ $(5.77 \times 10^{-5}\text{L}) \times 10^3 = 5.77 \times 10^{(-5+3)}\text{mL} = 5.77 \times 10^{-2}\text{mL}$
    Solution: $5.77 \times 10^{-2}\text{mL}$
  12. $25000\text{mm}^2$ into $\text{m}^2$
    $1\text{m} = 1000\text{mm} = 10^3\text{mm}$ $1\text{m}^2 = (10^3\text{mm})^2 = 10^6\text{mm}^2$ $25000\text{mm}^2 \div 10^6 = 25000 \times 10^{-6}\text{m}^2 = 2.5 \times 10^4 \times 10^{-6}\text{m}^2 = 2.5 \times 10^{-2}\text{m}^2$ Or as a decimal: $0.025\text{m}^2$
    Solution: $2.5 \times 10^{-2}\text{m}^2$ or 0.025$\text{m}^2$
  13. $60\text{m}^2$ into $\text{cm}^2$
    $1\text{m} = 100\text{cm} = 10^2\text{cm}$ $1\text{m}^2 = (10^2\text{cm})^2 = 10^4\text{cm}^2$ $60\text{m}^2 \times 10^4 = 6 \times 10^1 \times 10^4\text{cm}^2 = 6 \times 10^5\text{cm}^2$ Or as a number: $600000\text{cm}^2$
    Solution: $6 \times 10^5\text{cm}^2$ or 600000$\text{cm}^2$
  14. $2.7 \times 10^{12} \text{m}^2$ into $\text{km}^2$
    $1\text{km} = 1000\text{m} = 10^3\text{m}$ $1\text{km}^2 = (10^3\text{m})^2 = 10^6\text{m}^2$ $(2.7 \times 10^{12}\text{m}^2) \div 10^6 = 2.7 \times 10^{(12-6)}\text{km}^2 = 2.7 \times 10^6\text{km}^2$
    Solution: $2.7 \times 10^6\text{km}^2$
  15. $4 \times 10^9 \text{mm}^3$ into $\text{m}^3$
    $1\text{m} = 1000\text{mm} = 10^3\text{mm}$ $1\text{m}^3 = (10^3\text{mm})^3 = 10^9\text{mm}^3$ $(4 \times 10^9\text{mm}^3) \div 10^9 = 4 \times 10^{(9-9)}\text{m}^3 = 4 \times 10^0\text{m}^3 = 4\text{m}^3$
    Solution: 4$\text{m}^3$
  16. $5.1 \times 10^{-5} \text{km}^3$ into $\text{mm}^3$
    $1\text{km} = 10^6\text{mm}$ $1\text{km}^3 = (10^6\text{mm})^3 = 10^{18}\text{mm}^3$ $(5.1 \times 10^{-5}\text{km}^3) \times 10^{18} = 5.1 \times 10^{(-5+18)}\text{mm}^3 = 5.1 \times 10^{13}\text{mm}^3$
    Solution: $5.1 \times 10^{13}\text{mm}^3$

Further Pharmacy Calculations

  1. If you had $250\text{g}$ of a $18\%$ w/w cream, how many grams of active ingredient would be present?
    $18\%$ w/w means $18\text{g}$ of active ingredient per $100\text{g}$ of cream. Amount of active ingredient = $\frac{18}{100} \times 250\text{g} = 45\text{g}$
    Solution: 45 g
  2. If you had $600\text{mL}$ of a $6\%$ w/v solution, how many grams of active ingredient would be present?
    $6\%$ w/v means $6\text{g}$ of active ingredient per $100\text{mL}$ of solution. Amount of active ingredient = $\frac{6}{100} \times 600\text{mL} = 36\text{g}$
    Solution: 36 g
  3. If you had $12\text{mL}$ of a $4\%$ w/v solution, how many milligrams of active ingredient would be present?
    $4\%$ w/v means $4\text{g}$ of active ingredient per $100\text{mL}$ of solution. Amount of active ingredient in $12\text{mL}$: $(4\text{g} \div 100\text{mL}) \times 12\text{mL} = 0.04\text{g/mL} \times 12\text{mL} = 0.48\text{g}$ Convert to milligrams: $0.48\text{g} \times 1000\text{mg/g} = 480\text{mg}$
    Solution: 480 mg
  4. If you had $10\text{g}$ of a $2.5\%$ w/w cream, how many milligrams of active ingredient would be present?
    $2.5\%$ w/w means $2.5\text{g}$ of active ingredient per $100\text{g}$ of cream. Amount of active ingredient = $\frac{2.5}{100} \times 10\text{g} = 0.25\text{g}$ Convert to milligrams: $0.25\text{g} \times 1000\text{mg/g} = 250\text{mg}$
    Solution: 250 mg
  5. How many grams of sucrose (MW = $342$) are required to produce $200$ millilitres of a $2$ molar solution?
    Molarity (M) = Moles (mol) / Volume (L) Volume in Litres: $200\text{mL} \div 1000 = 0.2\text{L}$ Moles required = Molarity $\times$ Volume (L) = $2\text{mol/L} \times 0.2\text{L} = 0.4\text{mol}$ Grams required = Moles $\times$ Molecular Weight = $0.4\text{mol} \times 342\text{g/mol} = 136.8\text{g}$
    Solution: 136.80 g
  6. How many grams of glucose (MW = $180.2$) are required to produce $450$ millilitres of a $5$ molar solution?
    Volume in Litres: $450\text{mL} \div 1000 = 0.45\text{L}$ Moles required = Molarity $\times$ Volume (L) = $5\text{mol/L} \times 0.45\text{L} = 2.25\text{mol}$ Grams required = Moles $\times$ Molecular Weight = $2.25\text{mol} \times 180.2\text{g/mol} = 405.45\text{g}$
    Solution: 405.45 g
  7. A solution is $4\%$ w/v. $500\text{mL}$ of water is added to $1500\text{mL}$ of this solution. What is the resulting strength, expressed as $\%$ w/v?
    Initial concentration: $4\%$ w/v means $4\text{g}$ per $100\text{mL}$. Amount of solute in $1500\text{mL}$: $(4\text{g} \div 100\text{mL}) \times 1500\text{mL} = 60\text{g}$ New total volume: $1500\text{mL} + 500\text{mL} = 2000\text{mL}$ New concentration: $(60\text{g} \div 2000\text{mL}) \times 100\% = 3\%$ w/v
    Solution: 3.00% w/v
  8. A solution contains $80\text{mg}$ of solute per $\text{mL}$ of solution. $200\text{mL}$ of this solution is diluted by adding $300\text{mL}$ of water. What is the resulting strength, expressed as $\%$ w/v?
    Initial amount of solute in $200\text{mL}$: $80\text{mg/mL} \times 200\text{mL} = 16000\text{mg}$ Convert to grams: $16000\text{mg} \div 1000 = 16\text{g}$ New total volume: $200\text{mL} + 300\text{mL} = 500\text{mL}$ New concentration: $(16\text{g} \div 500\text{mL}) \times 100\% = 3.2\%$ w/v
    Solution: 3.20% w/v
  9. A solution contains $2.5\text{g}$ of solute per $100\text{mL}$ of solution. $500\text{mL}$ of this solution is diluted by adding $300\text{mL}$ of water. What is the resulting strength, expressed as:
    • $\text{mg/mL}$
    • $\%$ w/v
    Initial amount of solute in $500\text{mL}$: $(2.5\text{g} \div 100\text{mL}) \times 500\text{mL} = 12.5\text{g}$ New total volume: $500\text{mL} + 300\text{mL} = 800\text{mL}$ For $\text{mg/mL}$: $12.5\text{g} = 12500\text{mg}$ Concentration = $12500\text{mg} \div 800\text{mL} = 15.625\text{mg/mL}$
    Solution (mg/mL): 15.63 mg/mL For $\%$ w/v: Concentration = $(12.5\text{g} \div 800\text{mL}) \times 100\% = 1.5625\%$ w/v
    Solution (% w/v): 1.56% w/v
  10. The body mass index (BMI) is calculated using the formula: $\text{BMI} = \frac{\text{Mass}(\text{Kg})}{(\text{Height}(\text{m}))^2}$
    Calculate the BMI of the following patients:
    • Mass = $70\text{kg}$, height = $2\text{m}$
    • Mass = $49\text{kg}$, height = $140\text{cm}$
    • Mass = $51.2\text{kg}$, height = $160\text{cm}$
    For Mass = $70\text{kg}$, height = $2\text{m}$: $\text{BMI} = \frac{70\text{kg}}{(2\text{m})^2} = \frac{70}{4}\text{kg/m}^2 = 17.5\text{kg/m}^2$
    Solution: 17.50 kg/m$^2$ For Mass = $49\text{kg}$, height = $140\text{cm}$: Convert height to m: $140\text{cm} = 1.4\text{m}$ $\text{BMI} = \frac{49\text{kg}}{(1.4\text{m})^2} = \frac{49}{1.96}\text{kg/m}^2 = 25\text{kg/m}^2$
    Solution: 25.00 kg/m$^2$ For Mass = $51.2\text{kg}$, height = $160\text{cm}$: Convert height to m: $160\text{cm} = 1.6\text{m}$ $\text{BMI} = \frac{51.2\text{kg}}{(1.6\text{m})^2} = \frac{51.2}{2.56}\text{kg/m}^2 = 20\text{kg/m}^2$
    Solution: 20.00 kg/m$^2$
  11. The body surface area (BSA) is calculated using the formula: $\text{BSA} = \sqrt{\frac{\text{Height}(\text{cm}) \times \text{Mass}(\text{kg})}{3600}} \text{ (m}^2\text{)}$
    Find the BSA of the following patients:
    • Mass = $60\text{kg}$, height = $1.8\text{m}$
    • Mass = $80\text{kg}$, height = $180\text{cm}$
    • Mass = $150\text{kg}$, height = $192\text{cm}$
    You can use $\sqrt{2} = 1.41$ and $\sqrt{3} = 1.73$
    For Mass = $60\text{kg}$, height = $1.8\text{m}$: Convert height to cm: $1.8\text{m} = 180\text{cm}$ $\text{BSA} = \sqrt{\frac{180\text{cm} \times 60\text{kg}}{3600}} = \sqrt{\frac{10800}{3600}} = \sqrt{3}\text{m}^2$ Using $\sqrt{3} = 1.73$: $\text{BSA} = 1.73\text{m}^2$
    Solution: 1.73 m$^2$ For Mass = $80\text{kg}$, height = $180\text{cm}$: $\text{BSA} = \sqrt{\frac{180\text{cm} \times 80\text{kg}}{3600}} = \sqrt{\frac{14400}{3600}} = \sqrt{4}\text{m}^2 = 2\text{m}^2$
    Solution: 2.00 m$^2$ For Mass = $150\text{kg}$, height = $192\text{cm}$: $\text{BSA} = \sqrt{\frac{192\text{cm} \times 150\text{kg}}{3600}} = \sqrt{\frac{28800}{3600}} = \sqrt{8}\text{m}^2$ $\sqrt{8} = \sqrt{4 \times 2} = 2\sqrt{2}$ Using $\sqrt{2} = 1.41$: $\text{BSA} = 2 \times 1.41 = 2.82\text{m}^2$
    Solution: 2.82 m$^2$
